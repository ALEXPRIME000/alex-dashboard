# SECTION 1: RESEARCH & NICHE SELECTION

## 1.1 What Is a Dot Marker Activity Book?

A **Dot Marker Activity Book** (also called a "Do-a-Dot" book or Bingo Dauber book) is a children's activity book designed specifically for toddlers ages 2–5. The format is simple:

- Simple, cute illustrations are printed in black and white
- Large open circles (1–1.5 inch diameter) are placed **inside** the illustration at key points
- The child uses a **dot marker** (a.k.a. bingo dauber / Do-a-Dot marker) to stamp each circle
- Result: the illustration fills up with colorful dots, creating a satisfying completed image

**Why parents love them:**
- Develops fine motor skills
- Teaches color recognition
- Keeps toddlers occupied (quiet time)
- Mess-controlled (daubers are less messy than crayons for toddlers)
- Affordable and reusable concept

**Why they sell well on KDP:**
- Low content = fast to produce
- High demand (parents always searching for toddler activities)
- Low return rate (buyers know what they're getting)
- Easy to create series (multiple themes)

## 1.2 How to Find Profitable Dot Marker Niches on Amazon

### Step-by-Step Research Method

**Step 1: Amazon Search**
Go to Amazon.com and type the following search strings:
```

```

**Step 2: Analyze Top Results**
Look at the top 10–20 results per search. Note:
- Book title / subtitle
- Cover style
- Number of reviews
- Price
- BSR (Best Seller Rank) — found under "Product Details" on the listing

**Step 3: BSR Interpretation**
BSR (Best Sellers Rank) tells you how well the book is selling.

| BSR Range | Sales Estimate | Verdict |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| #1 – #5,000 | 20–100+ copies/day | 🔥 Hot niche — high competition |
| #5,000 – #50,000 | 5–20 copies/day | ✅ Sweet spot — profitable |
| #50,000 – #200,000 | 1–5 copies/day | 🟡 Possible — low competition |
| #200,000+ | <1 copy/day | ❌ Too slow |

**Target:** Find niches where multiple books sit at BSR #5,000–#50,000. This means consistent demand without being oversaturated.

**Step 4: Count Competition**
Search your exact target niche (e.g., "dot marker Easter toddler"). Count the results on Page 1:
- Fewer than 50 exact-match results = 🟢 Low competition
- 50–300 results = 🟡 Medium — needs strong cover + SEO
- 300+ results = 🔴 High — only enter if you can beat existing covers

**Step 5: Identify Gaps**
Look for themes that have demand (people searching) but thin supply (few good books). Signs of a gap:
- Search returns mostly coloring books, not dot marker specific
- Existing books have old designs / bad covers
- Seasonal niche with no recent book (e.g., Lunar New Year dot markers)

---

## 2.2 Target Audience Deep Dive

**Primary buyer:** Parent (mom age 25–40), grandparent, preschool teacher
**End user:** Toddler / preschooler age 2–5

**What parents are looking for:**
- Educational value (alphabet, numbers, animals)
- Quality illustrations (not clipart-looking)
- Right-sized circles (not too small)
- One image per page (not cluttered)
- Value for money

**Design decisions driven by audience:**
- ONE illustration per page (toddlers need focus)
- BIG circles (small hands, chunky daubers)
- Simple shapes (toddlers can't handle detailed art)
- Cute characters (animals with faces > plain shapes)
- Blank reverse side (prevents bleed-through from daubers)

---

## 2.3 Design Rules — Non-Negotiable

1. **ONE illustration per page** — never two
2. **Circles must be LARGE** — minimum 1 inch, ideally 1.25–1.5 inches diameter
3. **Circle placement must be INSIDE or touching the illustration** — not floating randomly
4. **Illustration must be simple** — 3–7 distinct shapes max
5. **Bold outlines** — minimum 3pt stroke weight on all outlines
6. **No fine detail** — toddlers are stamping, not coloring between lines
7. **Leave white space around the circle** — the dauber needs room
8. **The illustration must be recognizable** — animals should have clear heads/eyes/body

---

## 2.4 Circles Per Page Guide

| Page Type | Number of Circles | Example |
|-----------|------------------|---------|
| Very simple (single object) | 5–7 | One apple with 5 circles on it |
| Simple character | 8–10 | A cat with circles on body, head, paws |
| Medium complexity | 10–12 | A farm scene with barn, animals |
| More complex / scene | 12–15 | A car with wheels, windows, body circles |

**Rule of thumb:** If a toddler can finish the page in under 3 minutes, it has the right number of circles.

## 3.2 Method 1: Create Illustrations → Add Circles in Canva/Illustrator

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1: Source Your Illustrations**
Options (all commercial license compatible):
- **Canva Pro** — search "cute animal outline" or "kids coloring page animal"
- **Freepik** — search "toddler coloring page [animal]" (Premium license)
- **Flaticon** — outline icon sets
- **Procreate / Illustrator** — draw yourself if you have design skills

**Step 2: Prepare the Illustration**
- Convert to black and white if needed
- Remove background (make transparent PNG)
- Ensure all outlines are at least 3pt thick
- Scale to fit within a 7" × 9" safe zone on an 8.5" × 11" page

**Step 3: Add Circles in Canva**
1. Upload illustration to Canva
2. Set canvas to 8.5" × 11"
3. Place illustration
4. Add circles using the "Circle" element
5. Set circle: **no fill, black stroke, 3pt weight**
6. Size each circle to **1 to 1.5 inches (72–108px at 96dpi or 300–450px at 300dpi)**
7. Place circles logically inside/on the illustration
8. Export as PDF (Print, 300 DPI)

**In Adobe Illustrator:**
1. Place illustration on artboard (8.5" × 11")
2. Use Ellipse Tool (L) to draw circles
3. Hold Shift for perfect circles
4. Set Fill: None, Stroke: Black, 2–3pt
5. Use dashed stroke for a classic dot marker look (optional)
6. Group illustration + circles
7. Export → Save as → PDF (Press Quality)

## 3.5 Exact Circle Specifications

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Shape | Perfect circle (hold Shift when drawing) |
| Diameter | 1.0" – 1.5" (preferred: 1.25") |
| Fill | None (transparent center) |
| Stroke color | Black (#000000) |
| Stroke weight | 2.5pt – 3.5pt |
| Style | Solid stroke (classic) OR dashed (5pt dash, 3pt gap) |
| Minimum spacing between circles | 0.2 inches |
| Circles must overlap illustration? | Yes — placed inside/over the illustration area |

**At 300 DPI, 1.25 inches = 375 pixels diameter**

---

## 3.6 Page Layout Specifications

```
Canvas size:    8.5" × 11" 
Safe zone:      Leave 0.375" margin on all sides (KDP bleed/trim buffer)
Illustration area: Center of page, roughly 6.5" × 8" 
Circle placement: Within illustration bounds
Page number:    Bottom center, 0.25" from bottom edge (optional — KDP doesn't require it)
Font if using labels: Comic Sans, Fredoka One, or Bubblegum Sans — 18pt minimum
```

---

## 3.7 Interior Color: Black and White

**All interior pages must be strictly black and white for standard KDP printing.**

- No gray fills
- No gradients
- No color elements
- Outlines: pure black (#000000)
- Background: pure white (#FFFFFF)

If you use gray: KDP will print it as a dithered halftone, which looks dirty on cheap paper. Avoid.

## 4.1 KDP Cover Specifications

**Front Cover (if submitting full cover wrap):**

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Total wrap width | Front + Spine + Back |
| Bleed | 0.125" on all outer edges |
| Safe zone | 0.25" from trim on all sides |
| Resolution | 300 DPI |
| Color mode | RGB (KDP prints from RGB) |
| Format | PDF or JPEG |

**Front cover dimensions (standalone):**
- Trim: 8.5" × 11"
- With bleed: 8.75" × 11.25"

### Spine Width Formula
```
Spine width (inches) = Number of pages × 0.002252

Example: 64 pages × 0.002252 = 0.144 inches spine width

Full wrap width = 8.5 (back) + spine + 8.5 (front) + 0.25 (bleed both sides)
Example (64 pages): 8.5 + 0.144 + 8.5 + 0.25 = 17.394 inches wide × 11.25 inches tall
```

> Use the **KDP Cover Calculator** at kdp.amazon.com/cover-calculator for exact dimensions per page count.

---

## 4.2 What Sells: Cover Design Principles

**Must-haves on your cover:**
1. **Bright, saturated colors** — yellow, orange, red, teal, lime green. NOT pastel, NOT dark.
2. **The star illustration** — show the cutest animal/character from the book, large
3. **Visible dot circles on the cover illustration** — so buyers immediately understand it's a dot marker book
4. **Dot marker in hand (optional)** — show a dot marker next to the illustration
5. **Title in bold, chunky font** — must be readable at thumbnail size
6. **Age range visible** — "Ages 2–5" or "For Toddlers & Preschoolers"
7. **Series branding** — if building a series, consistent logo/banner

**Avoid:**
- White or pale backgrounds (disappears in search results)
- Small text
- Cluttered layouts
- Stock photo children (feels cheap)
- Complex scenes (thumbnail death)

## 5.1 Complete Page Order

```
Page 1 (Right / Recto):    TITLE PAGE
Page 2 (Left / Verso):     COPYRIGHT PAGE  
Page 3 (Right):            "HOW TO USE THIS BOOK" PAGE
Page 4 (Left):             BLANK (backs of activity pages are always blank)
Pages 5–74:                ACTIVITY PAGES (odd = illustration, even = blank back)
Page 75 (or last):         "MORE BOOKS BY [AUTHOR]" page
```

> **Pro tip:** Keep all activity pages on ODD (right) pages so the blank side is always facing away from the next activity. This prevents dauber bleed-through from ruining the next page.

**Total page count recommendation:** Aim for a multiple of 4 (e.g., 64, 68, 72, 76). KDP requires page counts divisible by 2; multiples of 4 are cleaner for binding.

## 7.2 Title Keyword Optimization

**Primary keyword must be in the TITLE** — Amazon weights title keywords most heavily.

**Formula:**
```
[Primary Keyword] + [Secondary Keyword] + [Audience]
```

**Good title examples:**
- ✅ "Animals Dot Markers Activity Book for Toddlers Ages 2-5"
- ✅ "Easter Dot Marker Coloring Book for Toddlers"
- ✅ "ABC Alphabet Do-a-Dot Activity Book for Kids Ages 2-5"

**Bad title examples:**
- ❌ "Fun With Dots! A Book for Kids" (no keywords)
- ❌ "The Ultimate Comprehensive Dot Marker Book Collection for Young Children" (stuffed)

**Subtitle strategy:** Use subtitle to capture secondary keywords and add a benefit statement.

---

## 7.3 Backend Keyword Strategy

7 keyword fields × 50 characters = 350 characters total.

**Rules:**
- No brand names (Do-a-Dot is trademarked)
- No competitor names
- No false claims ("bestselling" unless true)
- No repetition of title words
- Do use: synonyms, alternate spellings, related terms

**Research tools:**
- **Publisher Rocket** (~$97 one-time) — shows exact Amazon search volume
- **Helium 10** (free tier) — keyword research
- **Manual auto-suggest** (free) — as described in 7.1
