## SECTION 1: RESEARCH & NICHE SELECTION

### 1.1 — The Niche Profitability Framework

Not all niches are equal. You want niches where:
- **Search volume exists** (people are actively looking)
- **Competition is manageable** (not completely dominated by major sellers)
- **Buyers have purchase intent** (they're buying for themselves or as gifts)
- **The niche has emotional resonance** (identity, humor, occupation, life events)

The single most profitable formula in Merch on Demand:

> **[Occupation/Identity] + [Emotion/Humor] + [Occasion]**

Examples:
- Nurse + funny = "I'm A Nurse What's Your Superpower"
- Dog owner + humor = "My Dog Is My Therapist"
- Teacher + retirement = "Retired Teacher Loaded With Knowledge And Attitude"

### 1.2 — How to Find Profitable Niches

**Step 1: Amazon Auto-Suggest Mining**
1. Go to Amazon.com
2. Type in the search bar: `funny [keyword] shirt`
3. Note every auto-suggest result — these are real searches
4. Repeat with: `[keyword] t-shirt`, `[keyword] gift shirt`, `[keyword] tee`
5. Click top results — look at BSR numbers (see 1.4 below)

**Step 2: Google Trends**
1. Go to trends.google.com
2. Search your niche keyword
3. Check "Interest over time" — is it trending UP?
4. Check related queries — these are exact phrases people type
5. Set to "Past 12 months" and look for seasonal spikes

3. Sort by "Featured" first, then note BSR of top results

### 1.3 — Seasonal Calendar Planning (3-4 Weeks Ahead Rule)

**CRITICAL RULE:** You must have designs live 3-4 weeks BEFORE the holiday/event.

Why? Amazon's algorithm needs time to:
- Index your listing
- Serve it in search results
- Collect click data
- Build ranking momentum

For Etsy: Same rule applies, but Etsy has slightly faster indexing. Still aim for 3 weeks ahead.

**Planning timeline:**
- Event date: April 6 (Easter)

- Upload deadline: March 13-16
- Algorithm ramp-up: March 16 → April 6

For Christmas (Dec 25): Upload by November 25-30 at the latest. Top sellers upload by November 1.

### 1.4 — BSR Analysis for Merch (What Numbers Mean Sales)

BSR = Best Sellers Rank. Lower number = more sales.

**Amazon Merch T-Shirt BSR Benchmarks:**

| BSR Range | Estimated Sales/Month | Priority |
|-----------|----------------------|----------|
| 1 – 10,000 | 100+ sales/month | High demand — competitive |
| 10,000 – 50,000 | 30-100 sales/month | Strong niche — good target |
| 50,000 – 100,000 | 10-30 sales/month | Decent — worth pursuing |
| 100,000 – 500,000 | 1-10 sales/month | Low — only if niche is easy |
| 500,000+ | <1 sale/month | Skip |

**How to find BSR:**
1. Click on a Merch product on Amazon
2. Scroll to "Product Details" or "Additional Information"
3. Find "Best Sellers Rank"
4. Note the overall rank AND category rank (Novelty & More)

**Golden signal:** If you search a keyword and the TOP 5 results all have BSR under 100,000 — that's a validated, profitable niche. Jump in.

**Red flag:** If the top results have NO BSR listed, those designs have never sold. The niche is either too new or a dud.

## SECTION 2: THE SEO-FIRST DESIGN STRATEGY (CRITICAL)

> This is the most important section. Read it twice.

### 2.1 — The #1 Rule

**The TEXT in your design MUST match your TITLE, which MUST match what customers SEARCH.**

This is not optional. This is the entire business model.

The chain looks like this:
```
Customer types [phrase] in search bar
→ Your title contains [phrase]
→ Your design displays [phrase] visually
→ Customer clicks (because it matches exactly what they searched)
→ Amazon algorithm records: search [phrase] → click → purchase
→ Amazon promotes your listing for [phrase]
→ More organic sales
```

Break any link in this chain and you lose ranking.

### 2.2 — Why Text-Based Designs Outsell Pure Graphic Designs

**The psychological reason:** Customers searching for shirts are often buying to express an identity or message. They search for the MESSAGE, not for "a cool graphic."

**The algorithmic reason:** Amazon can't "see" your design image. It reads text: title, bullets, description. If your design text matches your listing text, the algorithm can confidently rank you for that phrase.

**The practical reason:** Text designs are:
- Faster to create (2-4 hours vs 8+ hours for complex graphics)
- Easier to vary (swap text, adjust colors)
- More scalable (one template → 20 variations)
- Higher conversion (customers immediately know what they're getting)

### 2.3 — How Amazon's Algorithm Works (For Merch Sellers)

Amazon A9/A10 algorithm for Merch works differently than regular Amazon products:

1. **No sales history** = No ranking initially
2. **Keyword matching** = Your title/bullets must contain the search term
3. **Click-through rate (CTR)** = Customers clicking your result signals relevance
4. **Conversion rate** = Purchases confirm relevance to that search
5. **Organic ranking builds** over 2-4 weeks of data collection

**Training the algorithm with ZERO sales:**

Before your first sale, the algorithm can only rank you based on TEXT relevance. This means:

- Title must contain the EXACT search phrase (not a synonym, not a variation)
- Bullet points must reinforce the phrase
- Design text must visually confirm the phrase (customer sees it and immediately knows)

The moment a customer searches → clicks → buys → Amazon locks in your ranking for that phrase. That's why the first 1-3 sales on a new design are disproportionately valuable.

**Niche:** Easter humor

```
Customer searches: "prepare to dye shirt"
→ Your title: "Prepare To Dye Easter Shirt Funny Easter Egg Dyeing Tee"
→ Design shows: "PREPARE TO DYE" in bold typography
→ Customer clicks (exact match to what they searched)
→ Conversion
→ Algorithm confirms: "prepare to dye shirt" → this listing

```

**What DOESN'T work:**

- Customer searches "prepare to dye shirt"

❌ Design shows an Easter bunny graphic with no text
- Customer can't tell from thumbnail what the shirt says

### 2.5 — Why Generic Titles Fail

"Funny Easter Shirt" fails because:
1. **100,000+ listings** have similar generic titles
2. **No phrase match** to specific searches
3. **High competition** on generic terms
4. **Low CTR** because there's nothing specific to click on
5. The algorithm has no reason to rank you over established sellers

**Specific beats generic every time:**
- "Funny Easter Shirt" → overcrowded, impossible to rank
- "Prepare To Dye Easter Shirt" → specific phrase, low competition, exact match

## SECTION 3: DESIGN CREATION

### 3.1 — Amazon Merch Design Specifications (EXACT)

```
Format:         PNG
Dimensions:     4500 x 5400 pixels
Resolution:     300 DPI
Background:     Transparent (checkerboard in editor = transparent)
Color mode:     RGB (NOT CMYK)
Max file size:  25MB
Color depth:    8-bit or 16-bit
```

**Critical notes:**
- NEVER submit CMYK — colors will shift dramatically when converted
- Transparent background is non-negotiable — Amazon places your design on the shirt color
- If your design has a white background, it will show as white on dark shirts (disaster)
- Keep the actual design centered and within the safe zone:
  - Top margin: ~400px from top
  - Bottom margin: ~400px from bottom
  - Side margins: ~300px from each side
  - Safe design area: ~3900 x 4700px centered

### 3.2 — Design Types That Sell (Ranked by Ease + ROI)

**Tier 1: Pure Text Designs (Best for beginners)**

Format: Bold statement text, centered or stacked
Example: "I Run on Coffee and Sarcasm"
Why it works: Fast to create, easy to keyword-match, high conversion

Typography rules:
- Use bold, readable fonts
- All-caps for impact phrases
- Mix font weights (bold headline + lighter subtitle)
- Leave breathing room (don't fill every pixel)
- 1-3 colors maximum

**Tier 2: Text + Simple Graphic Combo**

Format: Pun/phrase text + relevant simple icon/illustration
Example: "Prepare To Dye" text + Easter egg illustration
Why it works: Thumbnail stands out, text provides SEO, visual makes it shareable

Creation approach:
- Source simple SVG/vector graphics from Creative Fabrica
- Place text prominently (top/bottom), graphic as accent
- Ensure text is legible at thumbnail size (check at 150px wide)

**Tier 3: Word Cloud / ShapeCloud Designs**

Format: Multiple words/phrases arranged in a shape (heart, state, animal silhouette)
Example: Words describing "Nurse" arranged in a stethoscope shape
Why it works: High perceived value, gift-purchase trigger, visually impressive at full size

How to create on Creative Fabrica:
1. Log in at creativefabrica.com ($3.99/mo All-Access)
2. Search "ShapeCloud" in the tools section
3. Select or upload your target shape (heart, star, animal, etc.)
4. Input your word list (related keywords, phrases)
5. Adjust font, color, density settings
6. Export as PNG at required specs
7. Download and resize to 4500x5400 in Photoshop/Canva

**Tier 4: Minimalist/Trendy Aesthetic**

Format: Simple icon + small text, lots of white space, modern feel
Example: Minimal mountain range with "Adventure Awaits" in small type
Why it works: Broad appeal, works on apparel trend cycles

### 3.5 — Color Variant Strategy

**The rule:** Create one design, upload it across multiple color variants.

**Why it matters:**
- Different colors = different audiences (school colors, team colors, personal preference)
- Amazon counts each color as the SAME product (no additional design slot used at Tier 10/25)
- More color options = higher chance of sale

**Minimum color strategy:**
- 5 colors minimum per design
- Always include: Black, White, Navy, Dark Heather, Red

**Extended color strategy for occupation/school niches:**
Research school/team colors for your niche and include:
- Maroon (Texas A&M, Harvard)
- Royal Blue (Duke, Kentucky)
- Forest Green (Michigan State)
- Orange (Texas, Clemson)
- Purple (LSU, Northwestern)

**How to check which colors Amazon offers by product:**
1. Go to your Merch account → Upload
2. Select product type
3. Color options appear in the upload form
4. Note: Not all colors available for all product types

### 3.6 — What to AVOID (Critical Safety Rules)

**NEVER use:**

| Category | Examples | Risk |
|----------|---------|------|
| Trademarked phrases | "Just Do It", "Think Different", "I'm Lovin' It" | Immediate removal + account strike |
| Copyrighted fonts | Friends font, Disney fonts, Harry Potter font | DMCA removal |
| Celebrity names | Any living or recently deceased celebrity | Likeness/persona rights |
| Sports team names | NFL, NBA, MLB, NCAA teams | Heavy trademark enforcement |
| Brand logos | Nike swoosh, Adidas stripes | Immediate removal |
| Movie/TV references | "May the Force Be With You", "Winter is Coming" | Disney/HBO enforcement |
| Song lyrics | Any lyrics without license | Copyright violation |
| Political figures | President names, living politicians in negative context | Content policy + legal |

**Gray zones (approach with caution):**
- Generic holiday phrases (usually fine, verify on TESS)
- Common phrases in common usage (verify they're NOT trademarked)
- Historical figures (generally fine if educational, not defamatory)

### 3.7 — Trademark Checking Process (Before Every Upload)

**Step 1: TESS — USPTO Trademark Database**
1. Go to: tmsearch.uspto.gov
2. Click "Basic Word Mark Search (New User)"
3. Enter your phrase (e.g., "Prepare To Dye")
4. Set "Status" to "Live"
5. Search
6. If results appear: Check if the registered trademark is for CLOTHING (Class 025)
7. If Class 025 registration exists and is LIVE → DO NOT USE
8. If no results OR registration is in different class → likely safe (verify further)

**Step 2: Trademarkia.com (Secondary Check)**
1. Go to trademarkia.com
2. Search your phrase
3. Easier interface — shows same USPTO data with better visuals
4. Note registration dates and status

**Step 3: Google Search Check**
1. Search: `"[your phrase]" trademark`
2. Look for any company claiming the phrase
3. Also search: `"[your phrase]" shirt` to see if a brand dominates it

**Step 4: Amazon Brand Registry Check**
1. Search your phrase on Amazon
2. If one brand dominates ALL results with the phrase → they may have brand protection
3. This doesn't mean they're trademarked, but it's a warning sign

**Decision rule:**
- No live Class 025 trademark → PROCEED
- Live Class 025 trademark → SKIP (not worth the risk)
- Unsure → SKIP (protect your account above all)

## SECTION 4: AMAZON MERCH ON DEMAND — SETUP & UPLOAD

### 4.1 — Account Application Process

1. Go to: merch.amazon.com
2. Click "Request invitation"
3. Sign in with your existing Amazon account (or create one)
4. Fill out the application:
   - How you plan to sell
   - Your design experience/background
   - Website or portfolio (optional but helps — use a Canva portfolio)
5. Submit and WAIT
   - Approval time: 1 day to 6 months (completely random)
   - Most 2025-2026 approvals take 2-8 weeks
6. You'll receive an email when approved

**Tips to speed up approval:**
- Use a real Amazon account with purchase history
- Have an existing website or creative portfolio
- Answer the questions professionally and specifically
- If rejected, reapply in 30 days with better answers

### 4.2 — Tier System Explained

Amazon limits how many designs you can have live at once based on your tier:

| Tier | Live Designs Allowed | How to Reach |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| Tier 10 | 10 | Starting tier — just get approved |
| Tier 25 | 25 | Make 10+ sales from your first 10 designs |
| Tier 100 | 100 | Make 25+ sales while at Tier 25 |
| Tier 500 | 500 | Make 100+ sales while at Tier 100 |
| Tier 1000 | 1000 | Amazon invitation only |
| Tier 2000+ | 2000+ | Amazon invitation only |

**Tier-up strategy:**
1. Fill EVERY slot in your current tier
2. Focus on proven niches (high search volume, clear SEO)
3. Engage with current designs — add missing colors, refine bullets
4. Each sale accelerates the tier-up process
5. Amazon evaluates accounts monthly — patience is required

**Tier 10 strategy (critical starting phase):**
- You only have 10 slots — make them COUNT
- Research 10 different evergreen niches (not all the same category)
- Upload 1 strong design per niche to test which niches perform
- The niches that get sales = double down on when you reach Tier 25

### 4.3 — Upload Process (Step-by-Step)

1. Log in to merch.amazon.com
2. Click "Add Products" in the top menu
3. Select "Create" for a new design

**Step 2: Product Type Selection**
Options available:
- Standard T-Shirt (most popular, highest volume)

- Pullover Hoodie (great for fall/winter)
- Zip Hoodie
- PopSocket

- Tote Bag
- Throw Pillow

*Start with Standard T-Shirt. Add Hoodie once you have a winner design.*

**Step 3: Design Upload**
1. Click "Add front image"
2. Upload your PNG (4500x5400, transparent background)
3. Wait for preview to render (30-60 seconds)
4. Check: Does it look correct on the shirt preview?
5. Verify: No white background showing on dark shirts
6. Optional: Upload back design (usually skip for beginners)

**Step 4: Color Selection**
1. Select all available colors (minimum 5-10)
2. Check each color in the preview — especially verify design reads well on:
   - Black (most popular)
   - White (second most popular)

3. Deselect colors where design looks wrong (e.g., white text on white shirt)

**Step 5: Fit Type Selection**
Options: Men's, Women's, Youth


## SECTION 8: SCALING & AUTOMATION

### 8.1 — Batch Upload Strategy

**The batch mindset:** Never upload one design at a time. Batch everything.

**Weekly batch target by tier:**
| Tier | Weekly Uploads | Daily Design Target |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Tier 10 | Fill all 10 slots (first week) | 2-3 designs/day |
| Tier 25 | 5-10 new designs/week | 1-2 designs/day |
| Tier 100 | 15-20 new designs/week | 3 designs/day |
| Tier 500 | 30-50 new designs/week | 5-7 designs/day |

**Batch workflow for Amazon Merch:**
1. Design 5 concepts in one session
2. Export all 5 as PNG (queue them up)
3. Open 5 browser tabs with Merch upload page
4. Upload all 5 in sequence
5. Write all titles/bullets in a batch (copy/paste from your template spreadsheet)
6. Submit all 5 at once

**Time budget per design (batch mode):**
- Niche research: 20 min (covers 5 designs)
- Design creation: 45-90 min per design
- Upload + listing: 15-20 min per design
- **Total per design: ~75-120 min**

### 8.2 — Design Template System

**The master template approach:**

1. Create ONE strong base design (e.g., "Dog Mom" word cloud)
2. Save the editable source file (Canva/Illustrator)
3. Create variations:
   - Change main text only ("Dog Dad" instead of "Dog Mom")
   - Change sub-niche ("Golden Retriever Mom" → "Labrador Mom")
   - Change color scheme (same design, different color palette)
   - Change font weight/style (bold vs regular)
4. Each variation = new upload slot used

**Template variation formula:**
- 1 base design → 5-10 variations = 5-10 listings from 1 design session
- 1 base template → multiple niches = exponential output

**Color-only variations:**
- For Amazon Merch: Same listing, different colors (no extra slot used)
- For Etsy: Can create separate listings for dramatically different color palettes

### 8.5 — When to Remove Underperforming Designs

**Removal triggers:**
- Design has been live 60+ days with ZERO sales → Remove (free up slot)
- Design has been live 90+ days with <3 sales → Review (might keep if evergreen)
- Design received a warning from Amazon → Remove immediately

**Exception: Keep designs if:**
- They're evergreen (will sell at any time of year)
- You're at Tier 100+ (have enough slots to keep experiments)
- The niche is seasonal and season hasn't hit yet

### 8.6 — When to Double Down on Winners

**Double-down triggers:**
- Design achieves BSR < 50,000 → Create 3-5 variations immediately
- Design gets 5+ sales in first 30 days → Create color variants and related niches
- Design trending up in BSR → Promote it (Etsy ads, social media)

**How to double down:**
1. Create the same design in different color schemes

<a name="section-9"></a>
## SECTION 9: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY & ACCOUNT SAFETY

### 9.1 — The Non-Negotiable Rules

Your Amazon Merch account is your asset. Protect it above everything.

**One strike can:**
- Remove individual designs
- Restrict your upload rate
- Permanently suspend your account (everything gone, no appeal)

**The 3-second rule:** If you have ANY doubt about a phrase or design element — skip it. There are unlimited profitable ideas. Your account's safety is worth more than any single design.

### 9.2 — Comprehensive Trademark Research Process

**Before EVERY design upload:**

**Level 1: TESS Search (Required)**
1. Go to: tmsearch.uspto.gov
2. Select "Basic Word Mark Search (New User)"
3. Search the EXACT phrase
4. Also search key parts of the phrase separately
5. Filter results by "Live" status
6. Review any Class 025 (clothing) registrations
7. Note the filing date — recent filings may be pending

**Level 2: Trademarkia.com (Recommended)**
1. Search on trademarkia.com
2. More user-friendly — shows same data
3. Check "Dead" trademarks too — some sellers register and let lapse, then try to enforce

**Level 3: Google Full Search (Required)**
1. Search: `site:amazon.com "[your phrase]" shirt`
2. If one seller has 50+ listings with that exact phrase → they may have a brand protection claim
3. Search: `"[phrase]" + trademark`
4. Search: `"[phrase]" + copyright`

**Level 4: Amazon Brand Registry Check**
- Some phrases are protected via Amazon Brand Registry even without USPTO registration
- If Amazon's own search shows one brand dominating with that exact phrase → dangerous territory

### 9.3 — Copyright Issues to Avoid

**Copyright protects:**
- Original creative works (artwork, illustrations, text)
- Songs and lyrics (ALWAYS avoid)
- Movie/TV scripts and quotes
- Book content and characters
- Font designs (specific artistic implementations)

**NOT protected by copyright (public domain or not copyrightable):**
- Single words
- Short common phrases (unless trademarked)
- Facts and general information
- Fonts themselves (the letter shapes — specific digital font FILES are protected)
- Ideas (only the specific expression is protected)

**Safe zones:**
- Public domain art (pre-1927)
- Your own original creations
- Licensed content (verify license allows POD commercial use)
- Generic phrases (verify they're not trademarked)

**Fonts specifically:**
- Buying a font license from Creative Fabrica or similar ✅ (commercial license)
- Downloading a font from a free site without license ⚠️ (check license terms)
- Using "Friends font" or "Star Wars font" ❌ (these are trademarked/copyrighted)

### 9.4 — Amazon Content Policy Highlights

Amazon removes Merch designs for:

1. **Trademark/copyright violations** — most common
2. **Offensive content:**
   - Hate speech or slurs targeting protected groups
   - Sexual or explicit content
   - Violence glorification
3. **Celebrity likenesses** — even if no trademark
4. **False endorsements** — implying association with brands/organizations
5. **Political content** — not blanket banned, but highly scrutinized
6. **Drug references** — marijuana (even legal states), explicit drug use
7. **Misleading product claims** — "This shirt cures..." etc.
8. **Designs promoting illegal activity**

**Gray areas to simply avoid:**
- Religious content that could be seen as disrespectful
- Parody that too closely resembles the original
- Phrases associated with controversial figures
- Anything that required a second thought to upload

### 9.5 — Account Health Management

**Maintain a clean account:**
- Never upload anything you're unsure about
- If you get a removal notice, learn from it — don't push the same boundary again
- Keep track of all removal reasons in your spreadsheet
- Multiple removals in a short period = account review risk

**If a design gets removed:**
1. Remove any similar designs proactively
2. Document what the issue was (trademark? Copyright? Policy?)
3. Don't relist the same or similar design
4. Note the phrase/element in your "avoid" list

**Signs your account is at risk:**
- Multiple removal notices in 30 days
- A warning email from Amazon about content policy
- Sudden inability to upload new designs

**Best practice:** Run every design concept through the trademark check BEFORE creating it (not after you've spent 2 hours on the design and have to scrap it).
