### 1.2 How to Find Profitable Niches (BSR Analysis)

**The BSR Rule:** A book with a BSR under 100,000 in Books is selling at least 1–5 copies per day. Under 50,000 = 5–10/day. Under 10,000 = 20+/day.

**Research process:**

**Step 1: Search Amazon for your niche**
- Go to Amazon.com → Books → Search "word search for seniors" or "cat puzzle book for kids"
- Look at results on page 1 and 2

**Step 2: Check BSR**
- Click a book → scroll down to Product Details
- Find "Amazon Best Sellers Rank" → note the Books rank AND the subcategory rank
- BSR refreshes hourly — check 3–5 times over a few days for accuracy

**Step 3: Evaluate the competition**
- How many reviews does the top seller have? (If <50 reviews, you can compete)
- Are the covers professional or amateur? (Amateur covers = weak competition)
- Are there keywords in the title that aren't in others? (Opportunity)

**Step 4: Validate with KDP Rocket / Publisher Rocket**
- Tool: Publisher Rocket (~$97 one-time, worth it)
- Shows monthly searches for any keyword on Amazon
- Shows estimated monthly sales for any book
- Find keywords with 1,000+ searches and <5,000 competing titles

**Niche Sweet Spots (proven sellers):**

### 1.3 Age Targeting: Kids vs Adults

**Kids (Ages 4–12):**
- Simpler content, larger fonts, more white space
- More colorful covers (bright primary colors)
- Parents are the buyers → speak to parents in your title/description
- Gifting seasons drive big spikes (Christmas, birthdays, back-to-school)
- Pages: 80–100 (kids books can have more pages, parents perceive value)

**Adults / Seniors:**
- Large print is a key selling feature (mention it prominently)
- Clean, elegant covers work well
- Words like "relaxing," "stress relief," "brain training" convert well
- Series work well: "Volume 1, Volume 2" → loyal repeat buyers
- Pages: 80–120 (more content = justified price)

**Teens:**
- Trend-aware (include pop culture, gaming, anime themes)
- Sarcastic/funny titles work ("This Is Fine: A Puzzle Book")
- Bridge between kids and adult aesthetics

### 1.4 Seasonal vs Evergreen

**Evergreen (publish once, sell forever):**
- Animals (dogs, cats, dinosaurs, unicorns)
- Large print for seniors
- Bible/faith themes
- General brain training
- Occupations (nurses, teachers, firefighters)

**Seasonal (time-limited spikes, but predictable):**
| Season | Upload Deadline | Peak Sales |
|---|---|---|
| Christmas/Holiday | October 1 | Dec 1–24 |
| Halloween | August 15 | Oct 1–31 |
| Valentine's Day | December 15 | Feb 1–14 |
| Back to School | June 15 | Aug–Sep |

| Summer | April 1 | June–August |

**Strategy:** Build 3–5 evergreen books first (steady income). Then add seasonal books for revenue spikes.

## SECTION 2: CONTENT CREATION

### 2.1 Word Search: Tools + Manual Curation

**Free Tools:**
- **Discovery Education Puzzlemaker** (puzzlemaker.discoveryeducation.com) — free, web-based
- **Arroword.com** — online word search generator
- **WordSearchMaker.net** — export to PDF

**Paid Tools:**
- **Puzzle Maker Pro** — $39.99/year — exports to PDF, multiple grid sizes
- **Activity Book Creator** (Canva plugin) — integrates directly with design workflow

**Grid sizes for 8.5" x 11":**
- Kids: 15×15 grid, 15–18 words per puzzle
- Adults: 20×20 grid, 20–25 words per puzzle
- Large print: 15×15 grid, larger font (16–18pt)

**Manual curation — theme word lists:**
- Each puzzle needs a theme (animals, food, holidays, sports, etc.)
- Create your word list first (20–25 words), then generate the puzzle
- Include a title for each puzzle above the grid
- Always curate — generators sometimes create awkward or inappropriate hidden words. Always check the final grid.

**Tip:** Aim for mix of word directions: horizontal, vertical, diagonal, forward AND backward (for adults). Kids: horizontal and vertical only.

---

### 2.2 Mazes: Generation Methods

**Free Options:**
- **Mazegenerator.net** — generates mazes, exports to PNG
- **Psychedelic Mazes** (Python script) — for coders
- **Maze Generator by Mike Wiering** — multiple styles

**Paid Options:**
- **Maze Generator Pro** — $29/one-time, full control over size and complexity

**Maze specs for 8.5" x 11":**
- One maze per page (full-page = premium feel)
- Or 2 mazes per page (for easier kids' books)
- Complexity levels: Easy (wide paths), Medium, Hard (narrow paths, many dead-ends)
- Always include a START and END label
- Solutions section at the back

**Maze types:**
- Classic/orthogonal (most common)
- Circular
- Triangle
- Themed shapes (maze in the shape of a dinosaur, car, etc.) — premium feel, harder to generate

---

### 2.3 Crosswords: Tools

**Free:**
- **Crossword Labs** (crosswordlabs.com) — create and export
- **Puzzle Maker (Discovery)** — crossword option

**Paid:**
- **Crossword Compiler** — industry standard, $49–$99 — exports to PDF with professional formatting
- **Eclipse Crossword** — free for basic use

**Specs:**
- Grid: 13×13 minimum for adults, 9×9 for kids
- Always include a word bank for easier versions
- Clue numbering must match grid numbering exactly — always verify
- Answers in the back

**Tip for non-native English creators:** Use themed crosswords with simple, verifiable clues. Avoid trivia-heavy crosswords if you're not a native speaker — factual errors get bad reviews.

---

### 2.4 I Spy: AI-Generated Scenes

**What it is:** A busy, detailed illustration + a list of objects to find hidden within it.

**AI Tools for Scene Generation:**
- **Midjourney** — Best quality. Prompt: "busy children's bedroom, flat illustration style, vibrant colors, 40 small objects hidden throughout, children's book illustration"
- **DALL-E 3** (via ChatGPT Plus) — Good for specific theme control
- **Adobe Firefly** — Commercial-safe licensing (important for KDP publishing)

**Key Midjourney parameters:**
```
/imagine prompt: [theme] scene, I spy children's book illustration, 
flat vector style, bright and colorful, busy with many small hidden objects, 
white background, 8k --ar 8.5:11 --v 6
```

**Object list:**
- Create a list of 10–15 objects to find in each scene
- Objects must actually be visibly hidden in the image (check manually)
- Include some easy (big, obvious) and some hard (small, subtle) objects
- Format: "Can you find... ✓ a red umbrella ✓ a sleeping cat ✓ three blue stars..."

**Legal note:** Use Adobe Firefly or Midjourney (with commercial license) for any KDP-published content. Verify ToS before publishing.

### 2.5 How Many Puzzles Per Book

**Minimum viable:**
- 40 puzzles = too thin for most adult buyers, OK for kids sampler
- **50–60 puzzles = sweet spot** — justifies $7.99–$9.99 price point
- 80–100 puzzles = "mega" edition, can price at $12.99+

**Recommended count by type:**

| Book Type | Puzzle Count | Pages (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| Kids word search | 40–50 | 80–100 |
| Adult word search | 50–60 | 100–120 |
| Maze book | 60–80 mazes | 80–100 |
| Mixed activity (kids) | 40–50 activities | 80–100 |
| Crossword book | 50 crosswords | 100–120 |
| I Spy book | 20–30 scenes | 60–80 |

### 2.6 Solution/Answer Pages

**Always include answers.** Buyers complain loudly in reviews if there are no answers.

**Format options:**
1. **Separate answer section** at the back (most common) — label clearly: "ANSWERS — Pages 1–60"
2. **Per-puzzle answers** — answer immediately after each puzzle (wastes space, but reader-friendly)
3. **Upside-down answers** — printed upside-down below each puzzle (charming for kids)

**For word searches:**
- Print a small version of the grid with the words highlighted/circled
- Or just list the starting coordinates: "CAT: Row 3, Col 7, diagonal"

**For mazes:**
- Print a small version of the maze with the correct path drawn in

**For crosswords:**
- Print the filled-in grid at full size (smaller font OK for answer section)

---

### 2.7 Page Dimensions: 8.5" x 11"

**Why 8.5" x 11" (US Letter):**
- Most activity books use this size
- Larger format = more comfortable to work in
- Allows larger grids, more puzzles per page
- KDP prints this size — no trimming issues

**KDP specs for interior:**
- Trim size: 8.5" x 11"
- Interior margins: 
  - Inside (gutter): 0.75" (for books 100–150 pages)
  - Outside: 0.5"
  - Top: 0.75"
  - Bottom: 0.75"
- Bleed: Not required for B&W interior
- Color interior adds cost — usually stick to B&W for puzzle books

**Font sizes:**
- Puzzle titles: 14–16pt Bold
- Grid numbers: 8–10pt
- Clue text: 11–12pt
- Large print edition: Everything +2–4pt larger

## SECTION 3: COVER DESIGN

### 3.1 Cover Conventions for Activity Books

**Kids activity book covers (ages 4–12):**
- Bright, saturated colors (primary palette — reds, blues, yellows)
- Fun, playful fonts (rounded, chunky, not serif)
- Main subject: cute animal, cartoon character, or themed object
- Clear age range on cover ("For Kids Ages 6–10")
- Fun badges: "50+ Puzzles!", "Hours of Fun!", "Includes Solutions!"

**Adult/senior covers:**
- Cleaner, more sophisticated
- Softer color palettes OR bold monochrome
- Clear large print indicator if applicable
- Genre-appropriate imagery (nature, cats, coffee, etc.)
- "Volume X" series indicator for returning buyers

**Universal rules:**
- Title must be readable as a thumbnail (think Amazon search results)
- High contrast between title text and background
- Cover file: 2560px × 3360px (for 8.5"x11" at 300dpi — KDP requirement)
- File format: JPEG or TIFF (sRGB color profile)

### 3.3 Spine Calculation

**KDP spine width formula:**
```
Spine width = Number of pages × 0.002252 (for white paper)
OR
Spine width = Number of pages × 0.0025 (for cream paper)
```

**Example:** 100-page book on white paper = 100 × 0.002252 = 0.2252 inches spine width

**For cover wrap (full wraparound cover):**
Total cover width = Front cover width + Spine width + Back cover width
= 8.5" + [calculated spine] + 8.5"
= [total] inches

**KDP Cover Calculator:** https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/cover-calculators
- Enter: trim size, number of pages, paper type
- Download the exact template with spine marked

**For most activity books:** Start with front-cover-only (no wrap). Simpler, cheaper, faster. Use a plain or colored spine via KDP's automated option. Add a proper wrap when your book is validated and selling.

## SECTION 4: ASSEMBLY & FORMATTING

### 4.1 Page Order

**Standard activity book page order:**

```
1. Cover (separate file — do NOT include in interior PDF)
2. Title Page
3. Copyright Page
4. Table of Contents (optional for activity books — usually skip)
5. "How to Use This Book" / Instructions Page
6. Dedication (optional — adds a human touch)
--- CONTENT SECTION ---
7. Puzzles (organized by type or difficulty)
   - Page number on each puzzle page
   - Puzzle title/theme above each puzzle
--- ANSWER SECTION ---
8. "Answers" divider page
9. Answer pages (one per original puzzle page, or condensed)
--- BACK MATTER ---
10. "More Books by [Author/Brand]" page (with covers + Amazon links as QR codes)
11. Blank notes page (optional — kids like this)
```

---

### 4.2 Instruction Pages

**Keep it short.** One page maximum. Two versions:

**For kids:**
```
HOW TO PLAY!
🔍 WORD SEARCH: Find all the hidden words! 
   Words can go forward, backward, up, down, or diagonal!
   
🌀 MAZES: Help [character] find their way through the maze!
   Find the path from START to END.
   
💡 TIP: If you get stuck, answers are at the back!
```

**For adults:**
```
ABOUT THIS BOOK

This collection contains [X] [puzzle type] puzzles organized into 
three difficulty levels: Easy, Medium, and Challenging.

Each puzzle is themed around [theme]. Word lists and solutions 
are provided at the back of the book.

Grab a pencil, find a comfortable spot, and enjoy.
```

---

### 4.3 Difficulty Progression

**Structure:** Go from easy to hard. Never random.

**Three-section format:**
- **Section 1 (Easy):** 1/3 of puzzles — simple, shorter words, smaller grids
- **Section 2 (Medium):** 1/3 of puzzles — standard difficulty
- **Section 3 (Challenging):** 1/3 of puzzles — larger grids, longer words, harder clues

**For mixed activity books:**
- Group by type (all word searches, then all mazes, then crosswords)
- OR alternate types to maintain variety

**Visual separator pages between sections:**
- Simple page with section title: "LEVEL 2: MEDIUM 🔥"
- Adds polish, makes the book feel professional

---

### 4.4 Answer Key Formatting

**For word searches:**
- Print solved grid at 50% scale (4 grids per page maximum)
- Highlight/circle found words in a different shade (if color interior)
- For B&W: Print the grid with words circled using standard line circles

**For mazes:**
- Reduce maze to 50% size, draw the solution path in thick line
- Label: "Page [X] Answer"

**For crosswords:**
- Print filled-in grid at 70% scale
- Two crossword answers per page

**Header format for answer pages:**
```
ANSWERS

Page 5: Safari Adventure
[grid or solution image]

Page 7: Ocean Animals  
[grid or solution image]
```

---

### 4.5 PDF Specs for KDP

**Interior file requirements:**
- Format: PDF (PDF/X-1a preferred)
- Color space: Grayscale for B&W books (cheaper printing cost)
- Resolution: 300dpi minimum for images, 600dpi for line art (puzzle grids)
- Fonts: All fonts must be embedded in the PDF
- No crop marks, no bleed marks (KDP handles this)
- Margins: Per Section 2.7 above

**Software options:**

| Tool | Best For | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Adobe InDesign | Professional layout, templates | $25/mo |
| Affinity Publisher | InDesign alternative | $70 one-time |
| Canva Pro | Quick layout, no design experience | $15/mo |
| Microsoft Word | Basic interior (not ideal) | Free with Office |
| Google Docs | Very basic (not recommended) | Free |

**Recommended workflow:**
1. Generate puzzles in puzzle generator tool → export as PNG or PDF
2. Place in Canva/Affinity Publisher as individual pages
3. Apply consistent headers, page numbers, fonts
4. Export final PDF: "Print quality" settings, embed fonts, grayscale

**KDP file size limit:** 650MB for interior PDF. Puzzle books rarely hit this.

## SECTION 5: KDP UPLOAD

### 5.1 Title Optimization

**Formula:**
```
[Main Keyword] + [Niche Qualifier] + [Target Audience/Age] + [Volume Number]
```

**Examples:**
- "Large Print Word Search Puzzles for Seniors: 50 Fun Challenges — Volume 1"
- "Dinosaur Activity Book for Kids Ages 6–8: Word Search, Mazes, and More"
- "Cat Lover's Word Search: 60 Themed Puzzles for Adults Who Love Cats"
- "Halloween Maze Book for Kids: 70 Spooky Mazes for Ages 5–10"

**Rules:**
- Primary keyword in the title (not just subtitle)
- Include age range if kids/seniors
- Volume number if you plan a series
- Keep under 200 characters total (title + subtitle)
- Do NOT include: price, promotional text ("Best seller!"), author claims ("Amazing!")

---

### 5.2 Keywords

KDP gives you 7 keyword fields. Use all 7. Each can be a phrase (not single words).

**Strategy: Mix broad + specific**

**Broad (high volume):**
- "word search books for adults"
- "kids activity books"
- "puzzle books for seniors"

**Specific/Long-tail (lower volume, less competition):**
- "large print word search for seniors with dementia"
- "dinosaur word search for kids ages 6 8"
- "cat word search puzzles for adults"

**How to find keywords:**
1. Amazon autocomplete — type your topic and see suggestions
2. Publisher Rocket — shows actual search volume
3. Look at competitor book bullet points — they reveal their keywords
4. "Customers also searched for" at the bottom of competitor pages

**Never stuff keywords into your title/description unnaturally** — Amazon penalizes this.

---

### 5.3 Categories

KDP allows 2 categories at upload. You can request up to 10 via KDP support email after publishing.

**Best categories for activity books:**

For kids:
- Books > Children's Books > Activities, Crafts & Games > Activity Books
- Books > Children's Books > Activities, Crafts & Games > Puzzles & Games

For adults:
- Books > Humor & Entertainment > Puzzles & Games > Word Search
- Books > Humor & Entertainment > Puzzles & Games > Crosswords

For seniors:
- Books > Health, Fitness & Dieting > Aging > General

**To request additional categories:**
Email KDP support: "Please add my book ASIN [XXX] to the following categories: [list up to 8 more]"
They usually process within 3–5 business days.

### 5.4 Pricing: $7.99–$9.99

**Royalty calculation (KDP paperback):**
- Print cost (8.5"x11", 100 pages, B&W) ≈ $3.65
- At $7.99 list price: Royalty = ($7.99 × 0.60) − $3.65 = **$4.79 − $3.65 = $1.14/copy**
- At $9.99 list price: Royalty = ($9.99 × 0.60) − $3.65 = **$5.99 − $3.65 = $2.34/copy**

**Always aim for $8.99–$9.99** unless competing directly with a $6.99 bestseller.

**KDP Royalty options:**
- 60% royalty on paperbacks (standard)
- 35% or 70% on eBooks (for puzzle books, paperback is primary)

**Pricing ladder for series:**
- Volume 1: $7.99 (lower barrier to entry)
- Volume 2+: $8.99–$9.99 (once trust is established)
- "Mega" edition: $12.99–$14.99 (100+ puzzles)
