# Lead Magnet — Title Options & Book Blurb

## 📚 Three Title Options

### Option 1 (Recommended):
**The Grump Who Stole My Croissant**
*A Sugarcoat Falls Novella*

> **Why it works:** Immediately signals grumpy/sunshine + bakery setting. Witty and warm. Memorable. Trope-clear for romance readers without being generic. Strong shareability ("I need a man who steals my croissant").

---

### Option 2:
**Second Chances & Morning Buns**
*A Sugarcoat Falls Story*

> **Why it works:** Second chance trope is front and center. Bakery setting immediately clear. "Morning Buns" has a playful double meaning that romance readers will love. Good for subtitle searchability on Amazon.

---

### Option 3:
**Grumpy Baker, Sunshine Comeback**
*Love in Sugarcoat Falls*

> **Why it works:** Most trope-explicit option — grumpy/sunshine + second chance signaled immediately. Works well for readers who browse by trope. Less charming than Option 1 but highest keyword clarity.

## 📝 150-Word Book Blurb

**For Option 1: *The Grump Who Stole My Croissant***

---

She left Sugarcoat Falls at eighteen with a scholarship, a duffel bag, and a promise to herself she'd never look back.

Twelve years later, Ellie Marsh is back — just long enough to settle her grandmother's estate and sell the old Victorian she grew up in. It's temporary. It has to be. She has a life in Chicago. A plan.

What she doesn't have is any plan for Owen Calloway.

The grumpiest baker in three counties. Her old almost-something. The man who, infuriatingly, makes the best morning bun she's ever tasted and somehow knows exactly what she needs before she orders it.

Owen never expected her to walk back through his door. He definitely never expected to want her to stay.

But Sugarcoat Falls has a way of keeping the people who belong to it.

And Ellie Marsh might have left something behind that she can't put back in a box.

*A warm, witty second-chance small-town romance about showing up, staying put, and the person who was always worth going back to.*
